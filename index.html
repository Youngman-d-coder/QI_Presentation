What is Quality Improvement?

Understanding the fundamental principles of systematic healthcare enhancement

Quality Improvement (QI) is the systematic and continuous application of methods to improve healthcare processes, outcomes, and patient experiences through rigorous evaluation and evidence-based change.

Why Quality Improvement Matters

The strategic imperative for modern healthcare delivery

  • Improves patient outcomes and safety metrics
  • Reduces clinical errors and process variation
  • Enhances operational efficiency and workflow
  • Supports evidence-based clinical practice
  • Improves patient and staff satisfaction scores
  • Ensures regulatory compliance and accreditation

Sustaining Improvement

Long-term strategies for embedding excellence

01

Monitor Key Performance Indicators

Establish robust measurement systems with regular reporting cycles and clear accountability structures

02

Standardize Successful Changes

Document best practices, update clinical protocols, and ensure consistent application across all relevant units

03

Update Protocols and Workflows

Revise policies, procedures, and guidelines to reflect improved practices and eliminate outdated methods

04

Promote Culture of Learning

Foster psychological safety, encourage reporting, and ensure response to errors focuses on system improvement not blame

Quality Improvement should be integrated into daily practice, not treated as an additional burden

Continuous reflection, measurement, and adaptation drive better patient outcomes
